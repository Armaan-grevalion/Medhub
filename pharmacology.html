Pharmacology

Pharmacology is the study of drugs, their mechanisms, therapeutic effects, adverse reactions, metabolism, and interactions within the human body. It combines physiology, pathology, and biochemistry to understand how medications influence biological systems.

Explore Module Quick Revision

💊 Pharmacokinetics

Pharmacokinetics describes what the body does to a drug.

Absorption

Movement of a drug from administration site into circulation. Influenced by pH, blood flow, and formulation.

Distribution

Transport of drugs throughout body tissues and plasma. Protein binding affects free drug availability.

Excretion

Removal of drugs mainly through kidneys and bile. Renal clearance is clinically important.

⚡ Pharmacodynamics

Pharmacodynamics explains what a drug does to the body.

Agonists

Bind and activate receptors to produce a biological response.

Antagonists

Bind receptors but prevent receptor activation.

Therapeutic Index

Measure of drug safety comparing toxic dose to effective dose.

🧪 Drug Metabolism

Drug metabolism primarily occurs in the liver.

Phase I

Oxidation, reduction, and hydrolysis reactions mainly via CYP450.

Phase II

Conjugation reactions increase water solubility.

First-Pass Effect

Drug metabolism before entering systemic circulation.

🫀 Autonomic Drugs

Drugs targeting sympathetic and parasympathetic systems.

Beta Blockers

Reduce heart rate and blood pressure via β-receptor blockade.

Adrenergic Agonists

Stimulate sympathetic nervous system activity.

Anticholinergics

Block parasympathetic muscarinic receptors.

🦠 Antibiotics

Antimicrobial agents used to treat bacterial infections.

Penicillins

Inhibit bacterial cell wall synthesis.

Macrolides

Inhibit bacterial protein synthesis.

Aminoglycosides

Effective against gram-negative organisms but nephrotoxic.

⚠️ Adverse Drug Reactions

Understanding side effects is critical in clinical medicine.

Type A

Dose-dependent predictable reactions.

Type B

Unpredictable hypersensitivity reactions.

Drug Interactions

Can alter drug efficacy or toxicity.

📝 MCQs

Test your pharmacology concepts.

Which organ metabolizes most drugs?

A. Heart
B. Kidney
C. Liver
D. Lung
Correct Answer: C. Liver
Competitive antagonists primarily:

A. Destroy receptors
B. Increase efficacy
C. Block receptor activation
D. Cause irreversible activation
Correct Answer: C. Block receptor activation

📋 Clinical Case Study

Applying pharmacology clinically.

A patient with hypertension and tachycardia is started on propranolol.


Drug Class: Non-selective beta blocker


Mechanism: Blocks β1 receptors reducing heart rate and cardiac output.


Monitoring: Watch for bradycardia, hypotension, and bronchospasm.

💡 Clinical Tips

High-yield pharmacology principles.

Always learn mechanism + side effects together.
Know major CYP450 inducers and inhibitors.
Toxicity questions are common in exams.
Understand receptor physiology before memorizing drugs.
