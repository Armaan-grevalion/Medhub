Pathology

Pathology is the scientific study of disease, focusing on the causes, mechanisms, structural changes, and clinical consequences of abnormal conditions. It bridges basic science with clinical medicine by explaining how disease develops at cellular, tissue, and systemic levels.

⚠️ Cell Injury

Cellular injury occurs when stress exceeds the adaptive capacity of cells.

Reversible Injury

Includes cell swelling, fatty change, and membrane blebbing that can recover if the stress is removed.

Irreversible Injury

Leads to necrosis or apoptosis with permanent cellular damage and death.

Major Causes

Hypoxia, toxins, infections, immune reactions, nutritional imbalance, and genetic abnormalities.

🔥 Inflammation

Protective response involving immune cells, blood vessels, and molecular mediators.

Acute Inflammation

Rapid onset with neutrophil predominance and vascular changes.

Chronic Inflammation

Persistent inflammation involving macrophages, lymphocytes, and tissue destruction.

Cardinal Signs

Redness, heat, swelling, pain, and loss of function.

🧬 Neoplasia

Abnormal uncontrolled cellular proliferation forming tumors.

Benign Tumors

Localized growths that generally do not invade surrounding tissue.

Malignant Tumors

Invasive tumors capable of metastasis and systemic spread.

Hallmarks of Cancer

Sustained proliferation, angiogenesis, invasion, and resistance to apoptosis.

🔬 Cellular Adaptation

Cells adapt to stress through structural and functional changes.

Hypertrophy

Increase in cell size resulting in enlarged organs.

Hyperplasia

Increase in cell number due to hormonal or growth stimulation.

Metaplasia

Replacement of one mature cell type with another.

Atrophy

Decrease in cell size due to reduced workload or nutrition.

💡 Clinical Tips

High-yield pathology concepts.

Distinguish reversible from irreversible injury clearly.
Acute vs chronic inflammation is heavily tested.
Benign tumors may still cause serious complications.
Metastasis is a defining feature of malignancy.
