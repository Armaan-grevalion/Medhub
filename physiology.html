Human Physiology

Physiology is the scientific study of how the human body functions. It explains how cells, tissues, organs, and systems interact to maintain life, preserve homeostasis, and respond to internal and external changes. Understanding physiology forms the foundation of medicine, linking normal body function with disease processes, diagnostics, and clinical treatment.

⚖️ Homeostasis

Homeostasis maintains a stable internal environment through feedback mechanisms. Most physiological systems operate using negative feedback loops.

Examples include regulation of blood glucose, body temperature, blood pressure, blood pH, and fluid balance.

🧬 Major Physiological Systems

Each system contributes to maintaining life and internal balance.

Cardiovascular

Circulates oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and immune cells throughout the body.

Respiratory

Responsible for oxygen uptake and carbon dioxide elimination.

Renal

Controls fluid balance, electrolyte regulation, and waste excretion.

Endocrine

Uses hormones to coordinate long-term body regulation and metabolism.

Nervous

Enables rapid communication, reflexes, cognition, and coordination.

Gastrointestinal

Digests food, absorbs nutrients, and maintains metabolic support.

⚡ Core Physiological Mechanisms

Basic cellular processes that sustain life.

Diffusion

Movement of substances from high to low concentration without energy.

Osmosis

Passive movement of water across semipermeable membranes.

Active Transport

ATP-dependent transport against a concentration gradient.

Cell Signaling

Communication between cells through hormones and neurotransmitters.

🩺 Clinical Case Study

Apply physiology concepts to clinical reasoning.

Patient: 52-year-old male with fatigue, excessive thirst, and elevated fasting blood glucose.

Physiological Issue: Impaired insulin regulation causing disruption of glucose homeostasis.

System Involved: Endocrine system (pancreatic beta-cell dysfunction).

Likely Diagnosis: Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus.

Key Concept: Homeostatic imbalance due to hormone dysregulation.

🧪 Clinical Learning Tips

How to study physiology effectively.

Always identify the affected physiological system first.
Connect mechanisms directly to clinical symptoms.
Think in feedback loops and compensation mechanisms.
Use active recall and spaced repetition frequently.
Relate physiology to pathology for deeper understanding.
