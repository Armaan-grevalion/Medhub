Pulmonology

Pulmonology is the branch of medicine focused on the respiratory system, including the lungs, airways, gas exchange mechanisms, and respiratory disorders. Understanding pulmonary physiology and pathology is essential for diagnosing conditions such as asthma, COPD, pneumonia, respiratory failure, and acid-base disturbances.

🫁 Lung Anatomy

The respiratory system enables ventilation and gas exchange essential for life.

Trachea

Main airway conducting air from the larynx to the bronchi.

Bronchi

Branching airways distributing air into each lung.

Alveoli

Microscopic sacs where oxygen and carbon dioxide exchange occurs.

Pleura

Protective membrane surrounding the lungs and reducing friction.

🌬️ Gas Exchange

Oxygen and carbon dioxide exchange occurs by diffusion across alveolar membranes.

Oxygen diffuses from alveoli into pulmonary capillaries due to higher alveolar oxygen partial pressure, while carbon dioxide diffuses from blood into alveoli for exhalation.


Efficient gas exchange depends on:


• Intact alveolar membrane
• Adequate ventilation
• Proper perfusion
• Normal diffusion gradients

🧪 Arterial Blood Gas (ABG)

ABG analysis evaluates acid-base balance and respiratory function.

pH

Normal range: 7.35 – 7.45

PaCO₂

Reflects respiratory control and ventilation status.

HCO₃⁻

Indicates metabolic compensation and renal regulation.

PaO₂

Measures arterial oxygenation efficiency.

🩺 Common Pulmonary Diseases

Important respiratory disorders frequently encountered clinically.

Asthma

Chronic inflammatory airway disease causing reversible obstruction.

COPD

Progressive obstructive lung disease associated with smoking.

Pneumonia

Infection causing inflammation and fluid accumulation in alveoli.

Pulmonary Fibrosis

Restrictive lung disease causing reduced lung compliance.

B. Bronchi

💡 Clinical Tips

High-yield concepts for exams and clinical practice.

Always evaluate oxygen and carbon dioxide together.
Differentiate obstructive vs restrictive lung disease clearly.
ABG interpretation is a core clinical skill.
Ventilation-perfusion mismatch is a common exam topic.
