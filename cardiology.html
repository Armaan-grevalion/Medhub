Cardiology

Cardiology is the branch of medicine focused on the heart and cardiovascular system. It studies cardiac anatomy, electrophysiology, circulation, and diseases affecting blood vessels and the heart. Understanding cardiology is essential for diagnosing arrhythmias, myocardial infarction, hypertension, heart failure, and other cardiovascular disorders.

❤️ Heart Anatomy

The human heart is a four-chambered muscular pump responsible for systemic and pulmonary circulation.

Right Atrium

Receives deoxygenated blood from systemic circulation.

Left Atrium

Receives oxygenated blood from pulmonary veins.

Right Ventricle

Pumps blood to lungs via pulmonary arteries.

Left Ventricle

Thickest chamber responsible for systemic circulation.

🔄 Cardiac Cycle

Sequence of mechanical and electrical events occurring during one heartbeat.

Systole

Ventricular contraction causing blood ejection.

Diastole

Ventricular relaxation allowing chamber filling.

Heart Sounds

“Lub” = AV valve closure, “Dub” = semilunar valve closure.

📈 ECG Basics

Electrocardiography records electrical activity of the heart.

P Wave

Represents atrial depolarization.

QRS Complex

Represents ventricular depolarization.

T Wave

Represents ventricular repolarization.

Normal Rate

Typical resting heart rate: 60–100 beats/minute.

🩺 Common Cardiac Diseases

High-yield cardiovascular disorders encountered clinically.

Myocardial Infarction

Ischemic necrosis caused by coronary artery occlusion.

Heart Failure

Inability of heart to pump sufficient blood effectively.

Hypertension

Persistent elevation in systemic arterial pressure.

Arrhythmias

Abnormal heart rhythms due to electrical disturbances.

📋 Clinical Case Study

Applying cardiology concepts clinically.

A 55-year-old patient presents with crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm, associated with sweating and nausea.


Likely Diagnosis: Myocardial Infarction (MI)


Investigations: ECG and cardiac troponin levels


Immediate Management: Urgent reperfusion therapy (PCI)

💡 Clinical Tips

Important high-yield cardiology concepts.

Chest pain with diaphoresis should raise suspicion for MI.
Troponin is highly specific for myocardial injury.
Always interpret ECGs systematically.
Hypertension is a major cardiovascular risk factor.
